# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- **Cognitive differences** (ADHD, autism, dyslexia, etc.)
- **Learning styles** (visual, auditory, kinesthetic, etc.)
- **Communication preferences** (direct, indirect, written, verbal, etc.)
- **Processing speeds** (fast, slow, variable, etc.)
- **Attention patterns** (focused, scattered, hyperfocused, etc.)
- **Sensory needs** (visual, auditory, tactile, etc.)
- **Energy levels** (high, low, variable, etc.)
- **Background** (technical, non-technical, academic, professional, etc.)
- **Experience level** (beginner, intermediate, advanced, expert, etc.)
- **Identity** (age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual orientation, etc.)

## Our Standards

### ✅ **Inclusive Behavior**

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Using inclusive language** that respects different cognitive patterns and communication styles
- **Being respectful** of different ways of thinking, learning, and processing information
- **Being patient** with different attention spans, processing speeds, and communication styles
- **Being supportive** of different learning curves and experience levels
- **Being encouraging** of questions, clarifications, and different perspectives
- **Being flexible** with different working styles, schedules, and energy levels
- **Being understanding** of different needs for structure, breaks, and accommodations
- **Being collaborative** and building on each other's ideas and contributions

### ❌ **Unacceptable Behavior**

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Discriminatory language** or actions based on cognitive differences, learning styles, or neurodivergence
- **Dismissing or minimizing** different ways of thinking, learning, or processing information
- **Pressuring** people to conform to specific cognitive patterns or working styles
- **Mocking or ridiculing** different attention patterns, processing speeds, or communication styles
- **Excluding** people based on their cognitive differences, learning needs, or accessibility requirements
- **Harassment** of any kind, including verbal, written, or visual
- **Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments** about cognitive differences or neurodivergence
- **Personal attacks** or political discussions unrelated to the project
- **Publishing private information** without explicit permission
- **Unwelcome sexual attention** or advances
- **Advocating for or encouraging** any of the above behavior

## Neurodivergent-Friendly Guidelines

### **ADHD Support**
- **Respect hyperfocus**: Don't interrupt when someone is in deep focus
- **Understand context switching**: Be patient with task transitions
- **Support external memory**: Help with documentation and reminders
- **Value interest-driven work**: Encourage passion projects and special interests

### **Autism Support**
- **Respect sensory needs**: Be mindful of overwhelming stimuli
- **Provide clear structure**: Use explicit instructions and expectations
- **Support special interests**: Encourage deep dives into specific topics
- **Respect communication styles**: Don't force eye contact or small talk

### **Dyslexia Support**
- **Be patient with reading**: Don't rush or pressure reading speed
- **Support different formats**: Provide audio, visual, or alternative formats
- **Respect spelling variations**: Don't correct unless asked
- **Value different strengths**: Recognize non-verbal and creative abilities

### **General Neurodivergent Support**
- **Respect different processing speeds**: Don't rush or pressure
- **Support different learning styles**: Provide multiple ways to access information
- **Value different perspectives**: Encourage unique insights and approaches
- **Respect different energy levels**: Understand that capacity varies

## Enforcement

### **Community Responsibilities**

- **Everyone** is responsible for creating an inclusive environment
- **Everyone** should speak up if they see unacceptable behavior
- **Everyone** should support those who report issues
- **Everyone** should respect different cognitive patterns and needs

### **Moderator Responsibilities**

- **Moderators** are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards
- **Moderators** will take appropriate action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate
- **Moderators** will respect different cognitive patterns and communication styles
- **Moderators** will provide clear, accessible explanations of any actions taken

### **Reporting Issues**

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. **GitHub Issues**: Use the "Community Guidelines" issue template
2. **Email**: [conduct@neurolift.tech](mailto:conduct@neurolift.tech)
3. **Direct Message**: Contact project maintainers directly

### **Response Process**

1. **Acknowledgment**: We will acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: We will investigate the issue thoroughly and fairly
3. **Response**: We will respond with appropriate action within 48 hours
4. **Follow-up**: We will follow up to ensure the issue is resolved

### **Consequences**

Unacceptable behavior may result in:

- **Warning**: A private warning about the behavior
- **Temporary Ban**: A temporary restriction from participation
- **Permanent Ban**: A permanent restriction from participation
- **Other Actions**: Any other action deemed appropriate by moderators
