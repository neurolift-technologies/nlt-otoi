# Neurodivergent Support

OTOI is specifically designed to support neurodivergent users with built-in accessibility features and cognitive accommodations.

## What is Neurodivergence?

Neurodivergence refers to natural variations in human cognition, including:

- **ADHD** (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder)
- **Autism** (Autism Spectrum Disorder)
- **Dyslexia** and other learning differences
- **Dyspraxia** and motor coordination differences
- **Dyscalculia** and mathematical processing differences
- **Sensory processing differences**
- **Executive function differences**

## Core Principles

### 1. **Cognitive Diversity as Strength**
We believe neurodivergent thinking patterns are valuable, not deficits. OTOI is designed to support and amplify these patterns.

### 2. **User Agency First**
Neurodivergent users know their own needs best. OTOI puts you in control of how AI systems work with you.

### 3. **Accessibility by Design**
Accessibility isn't an afterthought—it's built into every aspect of OTOI from the ground up.

### 4. **Multiple Ways to Access Information**
We provide information in multiple formats and complexity levels to support different learning styles.

## ADHD Support Features

### **Attention Management**
- **Hyperfocus Support**: AI can recognize and support deep focus states
- **Context Switching Optimization**: Minimize energy costs of task transitions
- **Interest-Driven Workflows**: AI adapts to your natural motivation patterns
- **Attention Restoration**: Help you return to focus when distracted

### **Executive Function Support**
- **Task Breakdown**: Automatically break complex tasks into manageable steps
- **Progress Tracking**: Visual progress indicators and completion status
- **Priority Management**: Help prioritize tasks based on your energy and interest
- **Deadline Management**: Proactive reminders and deadline tracking

### **External Memory Systems**
- **Comprehensive Documentation**: Detailed notes and context preservation
- **Visual Organization**: Clear structure and visual hierarchy
- **Search and Retrieval**: Easy access to previously discussed information
- **Context Preservation**: Maintain project state across interruptions

### **Cognitive Load Reduction**
- **Clear Structure**: Logical organization with descriptive headings
- **Minimal Jargon**: Plain language explanations
- **Visual Aids**: Diagrams, charts, and visual representations
- **Multiple Formats**: Text, visual, and interactive options

## Best Practices

### **For Users**
1. **Start Simple**: Begin with basic configurations and add complexity gradually
2. **Test Incrementally**: Test each feature individually
3. **Customize Extensively**: Don't be afraid to customize for your specific needs
4. **Iterate and Improve**: Regularly update your TOI based on experience
5. **Share Experiences**: Help others by sharing what works for you

### **For Developers**
1. **Accessibility First**: Consider accessibility in every design decision
2. **Multiple Options**: Provide multiple ways to access information
3. **Clear Documentation**: Use clear, jargon-free language
4. **User Testing**: Test with neurodivergent users
5. **Continuous Improvement**: Regularly update accessibility features
