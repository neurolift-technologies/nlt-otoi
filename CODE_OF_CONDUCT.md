# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- **Cognitive differences** (ADHD, autism, dyslexia, etc.)
- **Learning styles** (visual, auditory, kinesthetic, etc.)
- **Communication preferences** (direct, indirect, written, verbal, etc.)
- **Processing speeds** (fast, slow, variable, etc.)
- **Attention patterns** (focused, scattered, hyperfocused, etc.)
- **Sensory needs** (visual, auditory, tactile, etc.)
- **Energy levels** (high, low, variable, etc.)
- **Background** (technical, non-technical, academic, professional, etc.)
- **Experience level** (beginner, intermediate, advanced, expert, etc.)
- **Identity** (age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual orientation, etc.)

## Our Standards

### ✅ **Inclusive Behavior**

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Using inclusive language** that respects different cognitive patterns and communication styles
- **Being respectful** of different ways of thinking, learning, and processing information
- **Being patient** with different attention spans, processing speeds, and communication styles
- **Being supportive** of different learning curves and experience levels
- **Being encouraging** of questions, clarifications, and different perspectives
- **Being flexible** with different working styles, schedules, and energy levels
- **Being understanding** of different needs for structure, breaks, and accommodations

### ❌ **Unacceptable Behavior**

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment** of any kind, including but not limited to sexual harassment, stalking, or intimidation
- **Discrimination** based on any protected characteristic or cognitive difference
- **Dismissing** or mocking different cognitive styles, learning needs, or communication preferences
- **Pressuring** others to work faster, communicate differently, or ignore their needs
- **Using** ableist language or making assumptions about cognitive abilities
- **Publishing** others' private information without explicit permission
- **Trolling**, insulting, or derogatory comments

## Neurodivergent-Friendly Practices

We specifically commit to:

- **Clear communication**: Using plain language, avoiding jargon without explanation
- **Multiple formats**: Providing information in different formats (written, visual, structured)
- **Flexible timelines**: Accommodating different working rhythms and energy levels
- **Explicit expectations**: Being clear about requirements, processes, and feedback
- **Sensory considerations**: Being mindful of sensory sensitivities in online interactions
- **Structured feedback**: Providing feedback in organized, digestible formats
- **Respecting boundaries**: Honoring communication preferences and interaction limits

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. **Opening a private issue** (if available) with the label `code-of-conduct`
2. **Contacting the project maintainers** directly via email
3. **Using GitHub's reporting mechanisms** for harassment or abuse

All reports will be handled with confidentiality and care.

### Consequences

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that is deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

**Enforcement ladder:**

1. **Warning**: A private, written warning with clarity about the violation
2. **Temporary ban**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community
3. **Permanent ban**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repository (issues, PRs, discussions, comments)
- Project documentation and wikis
- Community forums and chat channels
- Social media when representing the project
- In-person events (meetups, conferences, etc.)
